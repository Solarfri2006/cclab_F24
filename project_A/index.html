NEON GLOWLET

Scientific Name:

Neon Glowlet

Discovered by / in:

Dr.Hayley Yang, the year 3024.

Family:

Aquavioid family. This is named by scientists to refer to a family of artificially synthesized, high-tech BioProbes which intersect the bird species and the aquatic species.

Habitat:

Deep sea. Depth range 200~2000 m. Coral reef and deep-sea algae.

Appearance:

Key Characteristics:

  • Luminescent and irridescent in deep-sea.
  • Navigates underwater by flapping its wings.
  • Glides around like a jellyfish.
  • Swoops when hunting for food.
  • Feeds on harmful sea debris.
  • Tracks things as a deep sea probe.

What we know so far about Neon Glowlet? Our planet's oceans were becoming increasingly polluted with its acidification process intensifying and marine litter overflowing. Confronted with this senario, a group of Avant-garde scientists turned to developing bioprobes as alternatives for traditional probes. Their idea was to integrate different species' evolutionary advantages and high-tech to create probes with comprehensive functionality. Neon Glowlet is an owlet whose gene has been modified to include certain gene segments extracted from deep-sea jellyfish, allowing it to possess some jellyfish traits along with its own owl traits, such as being able to morph into a kaleidoscope of colors.In addition to its powerful recombinant genes, it is also equipped with a high-tech microchip, allowing it to function as a highly efficient probe accustomed to deep-sea surroundings.
Guidelines for human interaction with this creature:

  • When there's no movement from the audience, Neon glides around on its own accord.
  • Dragging the mouse continuously simulates Neon tracking something underwater (it follows the mouse smoothly).
  • Pressing any key would generate a random piece of debris in the water, after which Neon will swoop over and eat it.
